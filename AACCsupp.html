AI Readiness Assessment Framework

Use this framework to evaluate your institution's current AI readiness across five key dimensions.

1. Leadership & Strategic Vision

  • Does your institution have a formal AI strategy or position statement?
  • Is there executive sponsorship for AI initiatives?
  • Has AI been incorporated into strategic planning documents?
  • Are there dedicated resources (budget, personnel) for AI initiatives?

2. Infrastructure & Technology

  • Does your IT infrastructure support AI implementation?
  • Is there sufficient data storage and computing capacity?
  • Are data governance policies in place for AI use?
  • Is there appropriate cybersecurity for AI applications?

3. Policy & Ethics Framework

  • Are there policies governing ethical AI use on campus?
  • Do you have data privacy guidelines for AI applications?
  • Is there a review process for new AI implementations?
  • Are equity considerations explicitly addressed in AI adoption?

4. Faculty & Staff Readiness

  • What percentage of faculty/staff have received AI training?
  • Are there professional development opportunities for AI skills?
  • Is there a support system for AI implementation questions?
  • Are there faculty champions or early adopters leading innovations?

5. Teaching, Learning & Student Support

  • How is AI currently used in instructional contexts?
  • Are AI literacy skills incorporated into student learning outcomes?
  • Are AI tools used to enhance student support services?
  • How are students being prepared for AI in their future careers?

AI Literacy Program Components Framework

A comprehensive framework for developing AI literacy across your institution.

1. Foundational Knowledge Components

  • AI terminology and concepts
  • Types of AI applications relevant to community colleges
  • Capabilities and limitations of current AI tools
  • Ethical considerations and responsible AI use
  • Data literacy fundamentals

2. Stakeholder-Specific Modules

For Leadership:

  • Strategic implementation of AI across campus
  • Resource allocation and ROI considerations
  • Policy development and governance
  • Risk management and legal considerations

For Faculty:

  • AI tools for enhancing teaching effectiveness
  • Adapting assessments in an AI-enabled environment
  • Subject-specific applications of AI
  • Developing AI literacy among students

For Staff:

  • Operational efficiencies through AI
  • Department-specific AI applications
  • AI-augmented student services
  • Process automation opportunities

For Students:

  • AI tools for academic success
  • Critical evaluation of AI-generated content
  • Career preparation for an AI-integrated workforce
  • Digital citizenship in an AI world

3. Implementation Approaches

Delivery Methods:

  • Workshops and seminars
  • Online self-paced modules
  • Communities of practice
  • Certification programs
  • Embedded in existing courses

Program Phases:

  • Phase 1: Awareness building
  • Phase 2: Skills development
  • Phase 3: Application & integration
  • Phase 4: Innovation & leadership

4. Assessment & Continuous Improvement

  • Measuring literacy acquisition
  • Tracking implementation success
  • Gathering feedback for program refinement
  • Staying current with evolving AI technologies
