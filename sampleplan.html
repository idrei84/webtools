Institution Information

Institution: Lakeshore Community College

Prepared by: Dr. Emily Rodriguez, Dean of Academic Innovation

Date: April 14, 2025

Section 1: Target Area Selection

Priority Area for AI Integration: Instruction & Curriculum

Rationale for Selection:

Our faculty have expressed increasing anxiety about students' use of AI tools for assignments while simultaneously showing interest in leveraging these same tools to enhance teaching. Student surveys indicate 72% are already using AI tools, yet only 18% of our faculty feel prepared to address AI in their courses. This disconnect presents both a challenge and opportunity. By focusing on instructional integration, we can transform potential academic integrity issues into learning opportunities while addressing faculty professional development needs.

Section 2: Current State Assessment

Existing Processes:

Currently, we have no formal AI policy for either instructors or students. A handful of faculty are independently experimenting with AI in their teaching, while others have created strict anti-AI policies in their syllabi. Our Writing Center has reported increased concerns about detecting AI-written assignments. The Teaching Excellence Center offers occasional workshops on educational technology but has not yet developed AI-specific programming.

Pain Points/Opportunities:

  • Inconsistent approaches to AI across departments create confusion for students
  • Faculty express anxiety about detecting AI-generated work and maintaining academic integrity
  • Students are using AI tools without guidance on appropriate use cases or ethical considerations
  • Time constraints limit faculty capacity to redesign assignments or learn new technologies
  • Increasing enrollment in our IT programs creates urgency for AI literacy

Available Resources:

We have a recently updated Learning Management System (Canvas) with integration capabilities. Our Teaching Excellence Center has one instructional designer and a $15,000 annual faculty development budget. The IT department has a small AI testing environment. Three faculty members have significant AI expertise and have volunteered to serve as department leads. We also have a $50,000 innovation fund from a recent state workforce development grant.

Section 3: Implementation Planning

Specific AI Solution/Approach:

We will implement a three-pronged approach:

  1. Create an "AI-Augmented Teaching Toolkit" with discipline-specific exemplars, assignment templates, and assessment strategies that faculty can customize
  2. Develop an "AI Literacy Module" for students that can be embedded in any course
  3. Establish a new "AI in Education" faculty learning community with stipends for participants who develop and share resources

Section 4: Success Measures & Evaluation

Desired Outcomes:

  1. 50% of full-time faculty will complete at least one AI professional development activity by end of year
  2. 25 courses will incorporate elements from the AI Teaching Toolkit in their syllabi/assignments
  3. Student AI literacy (as measured by pre/post assessments) will increase by 30% in participating courses
  4. Faculty confidence in addressing AI in teaching will increase by 40% (via survey)
  5. Academic integrity violations will decrease by 15% compared to previous year

Evaluation Method:

We will implement a mixed-methods evaluation strategy including:

  1. Faculty surveys before and after professional development activities
  2. Collection and analysis of course artifacts (syllabi, assignments) that incorporate AI elements
  3. Student pre/post assessments on AI literacy and responsible use
  4. Focus groups with faculty learning community participants
  5. Tracking of academic integrity cases related to AI misuse
  6. Course success rates in sections using AI-enhanced teaching vs. traditional sections

Potential Challenges & Mitigation Strategies:

Potential Challenge Mitigation Strategy
Faculty resistance due to concerns about academic integrity Frame approach as "AI-resilient pedagogy" rather than "AI adoption," focus on assessment design that leverages AI as learning tool
Technology access inequities among students Ensure all recommended AI tools have free tiers, create AI lab stations in library, develop mobile-friendly resources
Rapidly evolving AI landscape making resources quickly outdated Focus on foundational principles and adaptable frameworks rather than specific tools, establish quarterly review cycle
Insufficient time for faculty to learn and implement new approaches Provide stipends for participation, develop ready-to-use templates, offer course release time for faculty champions

Section 5: Sustainability & Scaling

Resources Needed for Sustainability:

To sustain this initiative beyond the initial implementation, we will need:

  1. Dedicated instructional design support (0.5 FTE)
  2. Annual budget of $25,000 for faculty stipends, training materials, and tool subscriptions
  3. Integration of AI literacy into First Year Experience and College Success courses
  4. Regular updates to the AI Teaching Toolkit based on emerging tools and practices
  5. Continued executive sponsorship and policy support

Opportunities for Expansion:

If successful, this initiative could expand to:

  1. Create a certificate program in "AI for Education" for faculty professional development
  2. Develop AI-enhanced student support services (tutoring, advising)
  3. Establish cross-institutional partnerships with other community colleges to share resources
  4. Create an "AI in Education" resource hub for the wider educational community
  5. Develop specialized curriculum for workforce programs in AI-adjacent fields

Knowledge Sharing Plan:

We will document our process and outcomes through:

  1. Quarterly updates to Academic Affairs leadership team and Academic Senate
  2. Creation of an open repository of successful assignments and approaches
  3. Bi-annual workshops open to all faculty and staff
  4. Presentations at regional and national conferences (including AACC)
  5. Development of a publicly accessible microsite showcasing our journey and resources
  6. Publication in community college journals to share lessons learned
