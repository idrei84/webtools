Parallelism Review

Also known as Parallel Structure

EAP 1540

What is Parallelism?

Each item in a list, series, or comparison matches the grammatical form of the others.

Items in a series must have the same grammatical structure to be parallel.

Why Use Parallelism?

✓ Good Parallelism

  • More effective writing
  • Easier to follow
  • Logical flow
  • Professional appearance

✗ Poor Parallelism

  • Unprofessional writing
  • Confusing meaning
  • Unclear message
  • Difficult to read

Basic Examples

✓ Correct: Steven is a father, a husband, and a businessman.

Three nouns

✓ Correct: Steven is tired, friendly, and silly.

Three adjectives

✗ Incorrect: Steven is a father, eating breakfast, and tired.

Mixed forms = bad parallelism

Coordinating Conjunctions

and, or, but

He's worked at an ice cream shop, a movie theater, and a retail store.

It could be my sister, my cousin, or me.

We enjoyed the book, but not the movie.

Paired Conjunctions

both...and | either...or | neither...nor | not only...but also

Either a roast turkey or a honey glazed ham will make a delicious Christmas dinner.

I not only asked for extra salad dressing but also requested extra crispy croutons.

Neither her parents nor her siblings were able to attend.

Types of Parallel Items

Nouns

TV, PlayStation, games

Verbs

walk, swim, run

Adjectives

intelligent, hardworking, impatient

Gerunds

cooking, eating, playing

Infinitives

to marry, have children

Phrases/Clauses

under the bed, behind the couch

Infinitive Parallelism

✓ Correct: She likes to exercise, cook, and sleep.

Don't repeat "to" - connect to the first verb

✓ Correct: The desire to marry and have children is strong.

Base form verbs complete the infinitive

Complex Parallel Structures

Nouns with prepositional phrases:

Either a cabin in the woods or a condo on the beach.

Nouns with adjectives:

She had curly blonde hair, bright green eyes, and a slim physique.

Verbs with objects:

They neither cooked anything to eat nor ordered takeout from a restaurant.

Parallelism in Comparisons

Items on both sides of "than" must be parallel

My car is bigger than her car.

They would rather eat Japanese food than Turkish food.

Running is more enjoyable than swimming.
