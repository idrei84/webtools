Identifying Misplaced and Dangling Modifiers

EAP1540

What are Modifiers?

Modifiers are words, phrases, or clauses that provide additional information about other words in a sentence.

Correct: The tired student fell asleep during class.

The word "tired" modifies "student" by describing the student's condition.

Correct: Walking to school, Maria saw her friend.

The phrase "Walking to school" modifies "Maria" by describing what she was doing.

Misplaced Modifiers

A misplaced modifier is positioned incorrectly in a sentence, making it unclear what it modifies.

Incorrect: I only ate two pieces of pizza.
Correct: I ate only two pieces of pizza.
Incorrect: The man was stopped by a police officer on a motorcycle.
Correct: The man on a motorcycle was stopped by a police officer.

Dangling Modifiers

A dangling modifier doesn't have a clear subject to modify in the sentence.

Incorrect: Walking through the park, the flowers smelled wonderful.
Correct: Walking through the park, I noticed the flowers smelled wonderful.
Incorrect: After studying all night, the test was easy.
Correct: After studying all night, Sarah found the test was easy.

How to Fix Modifier Problems

For Misplaced Modifiers:

  • Move the modifier closer to the word it describes
  • Place limiting words (only, nearly, just) directly before what they modify

For Dangling Modifiers:

  • Add a subject that the modifier can logically describe
  • Rewrite the sentence to make the connection clear
  • Change the modifier into a complete clause
