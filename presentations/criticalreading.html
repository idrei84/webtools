Fact vs. Opinion Review

Facts

  • Can be proven true or false
  • Based on objective evidence
  • Verifiable through research

Example: "The library closes at 9 PM."

Opinions

  • Personal beliefs or judgments
  • Cannot be objectively proven
  • Often contain value words

Example: "The library is beautiful."

Look for: Value words, "should/ought," subjective language

Practice: Fact or Opinion?

A) Valencia College has three main campuses in Central Florida.

B) Valencia College offers the best student support services in the region.

C) Students who attend community college save money compared to four-year universities.

D) Community college is the smart choice for most students.
Identify each as FACT or OPINION. What clues help you decide?

More Fact vs Opinion Practice

A) The cafeteria serves lunch from 11 AM to 2 PM.

B) Homework should be banned on weekends.

C) Professor Martinez teaches three biology classes this semester.

D) Online classes are boring and ineffective.
Which words signal opinions? What makes the facts verifiable?

Spotting Value Words

"The new student center cost $2.5 million to build and features a beautiful modern design with terrible parking that will create awful traffic problems for students."
Find the facts and opinions. Which words reveal the author's judgments?

Challenge: Mixed Statements

"According to recent studies, 65% of college students work part-time jobs, and this situation is terrible for their academic success."
Which part is fact? Which part is opinion? How can you tell?

Opinions That Sound Like Facts

A) It is a fact that students learn better in smaller classes.

B) Research proves that exercise improves academic performance.

C) Everyone knows that math is the most difficult subject.

D) Studies show that 70% of students prefer online learning.
Which statements are actually facts? Which are disguised opinions?

Propaganda Techniques Review

  • Bandwagon: "Everyone's doing it!"
  • Testimonial: Famous people endorse it
  • Transfer: Associate with positive images
  • Plain Folks: "I'm just like you"
  • Name-calling: Attack the opponent
  • Glittering Generalities: Sounds good, means nothing
All use emotion instead of solid evidence!

Propaganda in Action

"Join over 10 million satisfied customers who have discovered the secret to effortless weight loss with our revolutionary system!"
What propaganda techniques can you identify here?

Political Propaganda

"I'm just a working parent like you, trying to make ends meet. My opponent, however, is a career politician who has never held a real job and wants to waste your tax dollars on ridiculous programs."
Identify all the propaganda techniques used in this statement.

Common Logical Fallacies

Ignore the Issue

  • Circular reasoning
  • Personal attack
  • Straw man

Oversimplify

  • False cause
  • False comparison
  • Either-or
Poor reasoning that offers no real support for arguments

Spot the Fallacy

"We shouldn't listen to Professor Johnson's research on climate change because he drives an SUV to work every day."
What logical fallacy is this? Why doesn't this argument work?

More Fallacy Practice

"Ever since the new principal arrived, our test scores have dropped. Clearly, the new principal is the reason our school is failing."
What fallacy is demonstrated here? What other explanations might exist?

Circular Reasoning Challenge

A) "This law is necessary because we need it."

B) "This law is necessary because crime rates have increased 15% this year."

C) "Reading is important for education because educated people need to read well."
Which examples show circular reasoning? Which one provides real support?

Either-Or Fallacy Detection

"You're either with us or against us in supporting this new policy. There's no middle ground when it comes to protecting our community's safety."
Why is this reasoning flawed? What options does this ignore?

False Comparison Analysis

"My grandfather walked five miles to school every day in the snow, so today's students who complain about online learning are just lazy."
What makes this comparison unfair? How are the situations different?

Critical Reading Challenge

"Studies show that 85% of successful people wake up before 6 AM, which proves that early rising is the key to success. My opponent obviously doesn't understand this simple truth - he probably sleeps until noon every day like all lazy politicians."
Find: Facts, opinions, propaganda techniques, and logical fallacies in this statement.

Advanced Analysis Challenge

"Everyone knows that social media is destroying teenagers' minds - just look at how my nephew became antisocial after joining Instagram. Either we ban social media for minors completely, or we'll lose an entire generation to digital addiction."
How many critical reading issues can you identify in this argument?
