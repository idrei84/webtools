Quick Review: Transitive vs. Intransitive Verbs

Transitive: Takes a direct object

The chef prepared the meal.

Intransitive: Does not take a direct object

The students arrived early.

Only transitive verbs can be made passive!

3/24

Active vs. Passive Voice

Active: Subject performs the action

The researcher conducted the study.

Passive: Subject receives the action

The study was conducted by the researcher.

Formula: be + past participle (+ by + agent)

4/24

When Do We Use Passive Voice?

  • Unknown or unimportant agent
  • Focus on the action/result, not the doer
  • Formal, academic, or scientific writing
  • When the object is more important than the subject

The experiment was completed last week.

Mistakes were made in the analysis.

Passive Modals

Modal + be + past participle

  • Possibility: The problem might be solved.
  • Necessity: The report must be completed.
  • Advice: The essay should be revised.
  • Permission: The document can be shared.
20/24

Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Wrong: The meeting was lasted three hours.

✅ Correct: The meeting lasted three hours.

❌ Wrong: The accident was happened suddenly.

✅ Correct: The accident happened suddenly.

❌ Wrong: The food was tasted good.

✅ Correct: The food tasted good.

Remember: Intransitive and linking verbs cannot be passive!

21/24

Passive Voice in Academic Writing

Use passive when:

  • Describing research methods
  • Reporting results
  • Stating general facts
  • Focusing on processes

The data was collected over six months.

The hypothesis was tested using statistical analysis.

The results were published in a peer-reviewed journal.
